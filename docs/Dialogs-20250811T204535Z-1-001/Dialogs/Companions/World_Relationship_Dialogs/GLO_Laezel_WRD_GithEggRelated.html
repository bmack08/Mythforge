    Synopsis:

    Dialog with Laezel regarding the Gith egg.

    - Conversation about just taking it from the hatchery

    - Conversation after players give the egg to Lady Esther

    - If players kept it in Act3:
    - - Conversation about how the egg is doing. Reactivity based on whether it's in Laezel's inventory. Later in epilogue, if it was in her inventory, it would be hatched and she would be mentioning it.

    - If players lose the egg or get it destroyed before reaching Act3:
    - - Laezel is not yet attached to it, so she mentions that it wouldn't have survived anyway

    - If players lose the egg or get it destroyed after reaching Act3:
    - - Conversation about Laezel being mad at you for not taking good enough care of it

    - If players gave it to Lady Esther, after seeing the hatched gith in Act3:
    - - Conversation about what happened to the kid and how he turned out, depending on Laezel's current views and outcomes of the situation (boy attacked you and you killed him OR convinced him that you're good or he's flawed in his beliefs and he didn't attack you)

    Follow up in Epilogue:
    - If Laezel kept the egg, it hatches and she can talk more about it
    - If players kept the egg or left it in camp, it hatches and we get a Gazette update about it
    - If players destroyed or misplaced the egg, it's not mentioned in the epilogue

    How to trigger:

    Multiple ways organically. The different branches trigger at different times. Usually by asking a question in the question bank, but obtaining the egg, giving the egg to Lady Esther and dealing with the hatched Gith kid in Act3 will also prompt an exclamation mark for a time.
